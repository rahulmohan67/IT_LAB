Onam

One of the most important and famous festivals is the Onam festival of Kerala. It is the harvest festival of Kerala that falls in the Malayalam month of Chingam. It is celebrated to commemorate the Vamana avatar of Lord Vishnu and the homecoming of King Mahabali. Around Onam, the whole state gets drenched in the festive spirit, marked with delicious dishes, dance, music, rituals and boat races. It is also the state festival of Kerala.

Christmas

In the state of Kerala, where Christians account for over a quarter of the population, Christmas holds a strong position. With festivities ranging from shopping and tree decorations to carols and feast, Christmas is definitely one of the most celebrated festivals in Kerala. The festival marks the birth of Jesus Christ.

Vishu

Vishu marks the beginning of Malayalam year and is one of the most important and famous festivals of Kerala. Mostly celebrated at home, a pooja with the family and the evening feast are the highlights of this Hindu festival.

Easter

Easter is the oldest Christian festival, the Central tenet of Christianity is not the birth of Jesus, however his resurrection. Easter stemmed from this paschal mystery and also from the occasions of Good Friday.

Ramadan

Ramadan is starting same day of the Islamic schedule, in this month Muslims around the world fast as well as worship God.

Muharram

Muharram is the first month of the Islamic Calendar. The full month of Muharram is spiritual according to the Muslims. Nonetheless, it is the tenth day which is of the majority of relevance. Different factions of the Muslim community observe now for different reasons.

Maha Shivaratri

Maha Shivratri- the night of Lord Shiva is a famous Hindu event commemorated with massive zeal and also excitement in different parts of the nation.The festival is usually celebrated in the month of Feb-Mar according to the Hindu Lunar Schedule.

Deepavali

The Festival of Lights is called Deepavali, this is the name of the festival in Southern India and this is just how the festival is referred to in other Oriental nations such as Malaysia as well as Singapore. In North India, it is more typically called Diwali.
